Welcome to the Project Z: Museum of Survival. The museum is divided into 4 sections. Details for each section can be viewed by clicking on the cards below or by clicking on the icons on the right (hidden for small screens).

At the beginning of the museum, you will have have the ability to choose a group to travel with and a docent character. The docent will guide your "survival" group throughout the museum. Of course, you can always go on your own if you choose.

Infrastructure

Infrastructure is the first group of exhibits you will encounter on your museum visit. Infrastructure is the underlying facilities, services, and installation required for a functioning community or society. In the case of a widespread emergency, most of the infrastructure in Los Angeles or your city will quickly collapse if proper measures are not put into place.

Power Exhibit

Which type of battery should you choose to be prepared for an emergency?

L.A. Exhibit

What can L.A. do to prepare infrastructure for an epidemic?

Communication Exhibit

Can you make the best decisions in an emergency situation?

The primary purpose of these exhibits is to help you prepare for infrastructure breakdown. After any emergency, government agencies and community leadership may not be ready in time to assist. In this case, preparing beforehand can greatly improve the community's chance of survival. Learn which type of battery is most suited for which situation and predict how long they will last. Also, see the optimal ways of interaction during times of distress.

Social

As you go forward, your group will enter the realm of social aspects for Project Z Museum. Social aspects will be the second group that visitors will encounter on the visit. Social in this case is the interpersonal communication and interaction between groups of people in an emergency situation. Knowing how to interact with those around you will be of utmost importance in times of disaster.

In this section, your group will be able to learn the proper measures to defend yourselves in a riot. Besides preparing for mass demonstrations, your group will be able to learn about communication, social distancing, and hysteria involved as well as the decisions that must be made when a disaster such as an epidemic or bioterrorism leaks to the public.

Medical

Take a right and your group will encounter the medical section of the museum. Medical refers to anything from prevention to wound treatment. Obtaining knowledge in the medical aspect of emergencies will greatly increase your chance of survival and allow you to assist others.

Control Exhibit

Is time to shut-down the virus for good.

Prevention Exhibit

How can you evade the epidemic?

Treatment Exhibit

Quickly! Fix the wound before you get infected!

In medical emergencies, your group will be informed on how to prevent and control epidemics as well as treat injuries induced by these disasters. Whether it’s the equipment used, treating wounds through cold compress/hydrogen peroxide, or analyzing wounds, this quadrant has covered and informed! Not only that, the exhibit group will provide calculations on the approximate supplies needed and graphs illustrating the rate of disease spreading using previous Los Angeles statistics on population as well as cover factors that contribute to previous epidemics. Last, but not least, medical emergencies will inform your group about disposals of contaminated equipment and portrayals of epidemics in American literature. All these information will not leave you vulnerable in emergency situations!

Survival

Walk straight forward and your group will enter the final quadrant: survival. In an emergency situation, survival refers to the set of skills necessary for self as well as group preservation. Your group will need the essential skills and knowledge taught in this quadrant to combat lack of order and lack of amenities.

Supplies Exhibit

Get the necessary supplies. Pack the supplies.

Skills Exhibit

It's more than just tying a knot.

Plan Exhibit

Prepare before catastrophe, not after.

The survival exhibits will provide tips for thriving in an emergency situation. This group will provide a list of items essential to a survival kit and expand on items such as heat packs and insulin. In addition to those items, this quadrant will share an evacuation plan that applies to most emergency situations. With these tips, your group will be able to survive for a prolonged period without the usual leisurely commodities used in daily life.

Power Exhibit

Flashlights, radios, and other electronic devices are crucial in emergency situations. However, they all require power. Some are rechargeable but others rely on batteries. This exhibit will explain the different types of batteries and their applications. Visitors will compute and measure amperage and determine how long batteries will last.
