Frequently Asked Questions

  • Museum

    Will I be able to interact with exhibits?
    Here at the Museum of Survival we strive to give our visitors as much hands on experience as we possibly can! Not every exhibit will be interactive, but rest assured that there will be plenty to do during your visit!

    Will this be a guided tour or can I go on a self-guided tour?
    There will be docents available who will gladly guide you around the museum and inform you about each exhibit. You can also choose a self-guided tour.

    When is the museum open?
    The visit page has this information.

    Will I need to pay admission?
    There is no need to pay for admission since the Museum of Survival is a nonprofit organization whose only goal is to raise awareness of emergency situations and prepare the public for any type of emergency.

    Do you offer tours in any languages other than English?
    Some of our docents speak multiple languages and will try their best to accomodate your needs.

  • Education

    What can I learn from this museum?
    The Museum of Survival will educate you about many emergency situations and what to do in case said emergencies do happen.

    What is so important about emergency preparedness?
    Being prepared for any type of emergency is crucial for the safety of those around you and yourself. Your chances of surviving a dangerous situation are much higher if you are prepared to deal with any emergency.

  • Rules

    Are pictures and videos allowed in the exhibits?
    Yes. Photography and video recordings are allowed in the exhibits.

    Can I eat or drink while in the museum?
    Food and drinks will not be allowed into the museum.

  • Directions

    How do I arrive to Museum of Survival: Project Z once I am on the California Academy of Mathematics and Science (CAMS) campus?
    Head towards the Engineering building and then go down the hall until you reach room 6020.
